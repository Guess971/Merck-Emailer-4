High HR intensifies mechanical stress on arterial walls,
accelerating plaque accumulation and atherosclerosis
progression.
These findings highlight the independent role of elevated HR in the
pathogenesis of atherosclerosis and underscore the potential benefits of
HR-lowering interventions in mitigating its progression.
